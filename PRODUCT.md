# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The site serves technical peers, open-source collaborators, researchers, professional evaluators, blog readers, and personal contacts. Visitors may arrive through a project link, a publication, a search result, a shared post, or a quick need to find a contact profile.

They are trying to understand who Jacob Xiaoyu Wei is, what kind of work he does, how rigorous and practical his judgment is, and where to read or reach him. The site should reward both a 20-second scan and a careful reading session.

## Product Purpose

This is a personal homepage, portfolio, and technical blog. It exists to present a compact public identity for a builder rooted in scientific computing, math, high performance numerical methods, and software craft.

Success means the page feels precise, credible, and distinct without becoming loud. It should make the work easy to inspect, the writing easy to read, and the contact paths easy to find.

## Brand Personality

Precise builder is the primary personality. Quiet scholar, eccentric technical, and minimal directory are supporting traits.

The tone is compact, rigorous, slightly personal, and uninterested in corporate gloss. It should feel like a carefully kept bench of tools and notes: sparse, useful, opinionated, and able to hold math-heavy writing without ceremony.

## Anti-references

Avoid SaaS polish: cream gradients, soft metric cards, generic startup sections, and AI landing page sameness.

Avoid academic CV dump: dense lists with no hierarchy, no rhythm, and no point of view.

Avoid designer portfolio theatrics: case-study spectacle, excessive art direction, and decoration that competes with the work.

Avoid terminal cosplay: monospace hacker styling, fake command prompts, and technical identity reduced to costume.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with proof, not persuasion. Publications, projects, posts, and contact links should speak plainly.
2. Keep the interface compact but not cramped. The site should scan quickly and still support long reading.
3. Preserve controlled weirdness. Small personal signals are welcome when they feel earned and specific.
4. Make technical depth readable. Math, code, citations, and long-form notes need first-class typography.
5. Prefer durable choices. The design should age like a reference shelf, not a campaign page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, semantic structure, keyboard navigation, and responsive behavior.

Respect reduced-motion preferences. Motion, when used, should be subtle, nonessential, and never tied to layout shifts.

Prioritize math readability on desktop and mobile: readable line lengths, scroll-safe equations, clear code blocks, and stable hydration.
